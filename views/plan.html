Location & Directions

The Duquesne Incline is located at 1197 West Carson Street, Pittsburgh, PA 15219, on the south shore of the Monongahela River. We’re just minutes from downtown Pittsburgh and accessible via public transit or by car.

Public Transportation

Several Port Authority bus routes stop near Station Square, which is a short walk across the Smithfield Street Bridge from downtown. Once you arrive at Station Square, you can take a short ride or walk to our lower station entrance.

Driving & Parking

From downtown Pittsburgh, cross the Smithfield Street Bridge and turn right onto West Carson Street. Follow the signs for the Duquesne Incline. Free parking is available in our lower station lot (space is limited, so arrive early on weekends and holidays). Paid parking lots can also be found near Station Square.

Hours of Operation

Monday – Saturday: 5:30 AM – 12:45 AM

Sunday: 7:00 AM – 12:45 AM

The last trip usually departs around 12:45 AM, so be sure to arrive in time if you want a late-night ride.

For school trips and organizations, see our Group Tours page to plan your visit.

Accessibility

Our upper and lower stations have ramps for wheelchair access, and our staff is happy to assist visitors with special needs whenever possible. To ensure a comfortable experience, please contact us in advance if your group includes individuals who may need additional assistance.

Best Times to Visit

  • Weekday Mornings: Typically the least crowded, making it easier to explore the machinery and museum displays.
  • Sunset & Evening: Many visitors come around dusk for breathtaking skyline views as the city lights begin to shine.
  • Weekends/Holidays: Expect more crowds. We recommend coming early or late in the day to avoid the biggest rush.

Field Trip Program

  • A free field trip program is available for students and youth groups of ten or more daily – except Saturdays and Sundays after 12:00 noon due to weekend crowds and limited space. The program includes:
  • A teacher's lesson packet for students' specific grade level
  • A round trip ride on the Duquesne Incline
  • A history of inclines in Pittsburgh
  • A discussion about Pittsburgh's industrial past
  • A visit to the machine room to observe the original hoisting equipment in operation and learn about the engineers who built it
  • A panoramic view of Downtown Pittsburgh and The North Shore from our observation deck 400 feet above the three rivers
  • A brief history of the French and Indian War from which the city arose
